# Show HN Launchpad

Turn shipped work into a launch kit that feels specific, credible, and easy to reuse across channels.

## When to use

- A new release, side project, or open-source tool is ready to share.
- The team has built something real but the launch message is fuzzy.
- A founder or maintainer wants a Show HN post without sounding like an ad.
- The same release needs tailored copy for multiple communities.

## Workflow

1. Start with the truth.
   - Identify what changed, who it helps, and what is genuinely novel.
   - Find the strongest "why now" angle.
2. Match the message to the channel.
   - Show HN wants the build story and sharpest demo.
   - Product Hunt wants value, audience, and polished positioning.
   - X wants short hooks and shareable lines.
   - Reddit wants concrete usefulness and less founder energy.
3. Draft the launch kit.
   - Create 3 to 5 title options.
   - Write the main post body.
   - Prepare likely comments, FAQs, and objections.
4. Keep it grounded.
   - Prefer specifics, screenshots, and lessons learned.
   - Do not fake demand or write in generic startup voice.
5. End with a launch checklist.
   - Links, screenshots, maker comment, docs, and fallback answers.

## Expected Deliverables

- Channel-specific title options
- A primary post draft
- Short-form variants for X or LinkedIn
- A comment and FAQ bank
- A launch-day checklist

## Output Style

- Crisp, concrete, and human
- Founder-written, not copywriter-written
- Honest about tradeoffs, limitations, and current maturity
